— 01 / About

Meet Alexis.

Alexis Frye is a 19-year-old engineering student, community organizer, and the reigning Miss Massachusetts Teen Coed. Her journey to the crown has been shaped by curiosity, persistence, and a lifelong love of figuring out how things work — even when the numbers refused to cooperate.

Diagnosed with numeric dyslexia, Alexis spent years convinced she could never pursue a STEM career. Today, she's proving the opposite — and using her title to make sure other students know that a learning difference is never the end of the story.

Beyond pageantry, she founded Grow with STEM, a community organization that brings hands-on science, technology, engineering, and math experiences to classrooms, libraries, and scout troops across Massachusetts.

— 02 / Platform

Grow with STEM.

A reign isn't required to have a cause — but mine has one. Through Grow with STEM, I share my story of overcoming numeric dyslexia and invite young people to discover that engineering, science, and curiosity are within their reach.

i.

Schools & Classrooms

Age-appropriate talks and hands-on STEM activities for K–12 — assemblies, single-class visits, and career-day appearances.

ii.

Libraries & Troops

Story-time STEM sessions, badge-aligned activities for Girl Scouts and Boy Scouts, and library-hosted programs that make discovery feel like play.

iii.

Learning Differences

Honest conversations about numeric dyslexia, the path through self-doubt, and how I turned a struggle into the reason I study engineering today.
